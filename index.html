Survey PeriodFiscal years 2017–2025 (B.E. 2560–2568), 9 consecutive years of longitudinal data

Peak Hour WindowsMorning peak: 07:00–09:00 · Evening peak: 16:00–18:00 (Bangkok local time, UTC+7)
Speed UnitAverage travel speed in km/h, recorded bidirectionally (inbound = toward CBD, outbound = away from CBD)
Travel Time CalculationDerived from average speed: Travel Time (min) = (10 ÷ Speed) × 60 · expressed as minutes per 10 km
Data SourceBMA Department of Traffic and Transportation (สจส.) — official government survey, not GPS-based

06 · Key Findings & Observations
Summary Insights from the Analysis
Based on 9-year BMA longitudinal survey (2017–2025) · 50 road segments · and TomTom Traffic Index 2025
⚠️
Bangkok Ranked 10th Most Congested Globally
TomTom 2025 records a congestion level of 67.9%, up 1.3 percentage points year-on-year. Evening peak congestion surges to 126.5%, with commuters losing 115 hours annually to traffic delays.
📉
Speeds Below 2017 Baseline Across All Zones
The outer zone recorded the largest decline, falling from 29.23 to 25.99 km/h (−11%), reflecting the expansion of suburban communities and growing vehicle volumes on peripheral roads.
🦠
2021: Highest Speeds in 9 Years (COVID-19 Effect)
Outer zone inbound reached 32.69 km/h due to significantly reduced traffic volumes during lockdowns. Following the pandemic, speeds have declined continuously back toward pre-COVID levels.
↕️
Outbound Consistently Faster Than Inbound
Morning peak: outbound is +5.8 km/h faster · Evening peak: +2.6 km/h faster. The directional speed gap narrows significantly in the evening, as outbound traffic increases after work hours.
⏱️
Inner Zone Evening Worse Than Morning
Inner zone inbound travel time reaches 41.1 min/10 km in the evening, compared to 39.3 min/10 km in the morning — indicating that central Bangkok experiences more severe congestion during the PM peak.
🔭
Continued Speed Decline Post-2021
Speeds have declined across all zones since the post-COVID rebound, highlighting the need for proactive traffic management policies and expanded public transit capacity to address growing mobility demand.
